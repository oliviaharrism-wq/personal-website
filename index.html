About me

PM at the intersection of data, strategy, and people

I'm a Senior Technical Product Manager at Nike, where I work on supply chain technology for the materials planning domain — helping Nike understand, plan, and optimize the materials that go into every shoe and piece of apparel.

What I do best is take a broad, ambiguous question — "how do we improve quality at Nike?" or "how do we improve the patient experience?" — and turn it into a concrete, executable plan. That means synthesizing external market data with internal insights, stakeholder goals, business capabilities, and company strategy to build a cohesive vision. I apply both business and technology frameworks rigorously: Jobs to Be Done, DVF (Desirability, Viability, Feasibility), MoSCoW, Cost of Delay, Impact vs. Effort, and RACI — choosing the right tool for the right conversation.

My path to product has been anything but linear. I started in client services, moved into business analysis, then into associate PM roles across healthcare IT at Phreesia, Reify Health, and Imagen Technologies. Along the way I developed a deep appreciation for the unglamorous work that makes great products possible: user research, financial modeling, cross-functional alignment, and clear writing.

At Nike, I've run end-to-end user research with 30+ senior leaders, built financial models that unlocked executive investment, and launched AI prototypes that reimagine how factories inspect quality. I care about shipping things that actually get used.

Outside work I play recreational volleyball, mentor early-career PMs, and am on a probably-too-serious matcha quest.
